What is 4C Hair?

4C is the curliest hair and indicates the tightest curl pattern. It features densely coiled natural curls with excessive shrinkage, and the hair strands are springy and tight. This type of hair can be coarse, thick, and fine, and the strands are prone to excessive shrinkage. This makes styling difficult; hence requires special tools like blow dryers exclusively made for 4C hair type. Below are some good blow dryers for 4C hair that you can try.

    How to Choose a Blow Dryer for 4C Hair?

    Here are a few points you can consider before stashing a new 4C hair blow dryer into your cart. 

    Technology 

    The best hair dryers for 4C hair are made with different types of technology. For instance, ionic technology adds negative ions to frizzy hair to control static and improve manageability, while leaving tresses smooth and shiny. Ceramic and tourmaline technology indicates the materials used. These materials allow even heat distribution and protect hair from damage. 

    Attachment 

    A diffuser with additional attachments like a comb, concentrator, nozzles, etc., simplifies styling. These advanced tools improve coily hair texture and feel. 

    Design 

    Pick a dryer that has a user-friendly friendly design and feels lightweight. You can check for features like anti-slip grip, safe plugs, long cords, etc. 

    Temperature And Speed Settings 

    Most blow dryers for 4C hair include multiple speed and temperature settings that let you adjust the heat and airflow based on the styling needs. Ensure the heat is neither too high nor too low, just fix the temperature at the right setting. This will protect your hair from heat damage. 

    Performance 

    The dryer's performance is typically associated with the motor's power. High-performing dryers generally include motors with a power setting of 1800 watts and above. They release a strong blowout to dry hair quickly. 

    In tandem, our contributor Kate Ross, a Hair Specialist at ‘Irresistible Me’ - a beauty brand specializing in premium human hair extensions and wigs, says, “The power of a hairdryer's motor affects its performance. A more powerful motor can provide stronger airflow and more heat, which results in faster drying times. However, for certain hair types, especially fine or damaged hair, a less powerful motor with adjustable heat settings might be preferable to prevent heat damage.”

    We hope these tips will help you find the aptest dryer. Below are a few hacks you can check out to protect your hair before blow drying. 

    How Do I Protect My 4C Hair Before Blow Drying?

    You can try the given suggestions to prevent your hair from damage. 

  • Cleanse and condition your hair first. Follow with detangling to prepare strands for blow drying. 
  • Moisturize hair with a leave-in conditioner and apply a heat protectant spray that will protect your hair from heat damage and add shine. 
  • Part hair in 3 to 4 sections and clip each section. Drying one section at a time ensures precise and even heating. 
  • Release tension from hair. Hold one section, straighten it gently using your fingers and run the dryer from top to bottom.

    • Sharing a tip to release tension from hair, our contributor Kate Ross, says, “4c hair type (known for its tight coils and susceptibility to dryness) can experience tension from certain hairstyles. To release this tension, regular moisturizing, gentle detangling, and protective styling are key. Using products with natural oils and butters can also help maintain the hair’s elasticity and prevent breakage.”

    After getting your hair ready for a blow dry, here's how you can follow the process correctly. 

    How Do You Blow Dry Natural 4C Hair?

    These are the steps to precisely blow drying hair. 

  • Use a comb or brush attachment to the dryer as it spreads heat evenly. 
  • Begin with a low heat setting. You can gradually increase the temperature and speed according to the requirements. 
  • Turn on the cool setting to seal the hair cuticles and lock natural moisture. 
  • Give a final touch-up with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to set the hairstyle.

    FAQ's

    Will Blow-Drying Damage My 4C Hair?

    Operating dryers on extremely high levels for a long duration may weaken strands and lead to hair damage. It's better to use them on the required levels.

    Can I Blow Dry 4C Hair Every Week?

    Typically, folks with 4C hair use the blow-drying method once a week for 30 to 45 minutes, depending on hair texture and length.

    Can a Blow Dryer Straighten 4C Hair?

    Yes, blow dryers with a comb, nozzle, and other attachments may help straighten 4C hair.
