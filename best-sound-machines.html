What’s the safest machine decibel/volume level for a sound machine?

The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency and World Health Organization recommend keeping environmental noises below 75 dBA (adjusted decibels) over eight hours—roughly a full night's sleep—to prevent noise-induced hearing loss. To properly measure your machine's volume level, you can download the NIOSH sound level meter app here.

Can using one of the best sound machines help with insomnia?

According to the Mayo Clinic, changing your sleep habits and addressing issues, like stress, that may contribute to insomnia is the best way to course correct it. "It’s not prescriptive, but many users find that sound machines can help mask external and internal noises, like louder family members or pets, which can disrupt sleep patterns," Sage + Sound programming director George MacPherson explains. Since the best sound machines feature soothing noises to help put your mind at ease, they're an asset in minimizing the effects of insomnia.
