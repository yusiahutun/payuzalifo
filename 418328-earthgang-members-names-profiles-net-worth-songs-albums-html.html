EarthGang history

The rap duo EarthGang was created in 2008 by Olu and WowGr8, who were both highs school students in Atlanta, Georgia. They first interacted during a field trip in 9th grade at Benjamin Elijah Mays High School. Venus later approached WowGr8 about creating a rap group.

According to an interview with Hucker magazine in 2018, the two members used to swap records while in high school.

We used to swap records at high school. We’d pull up and have a corner of the parking lot. Everybody knew not to come our way, not unless you were bumpin’ music out yo’ whip. One day we might be listening to Curtis Mayfield, next day, we might be listening to Nina Simone. There was a whole squad of us at the time, but we were the two who happened to rap and keep going with it.

WowGr8 and Venus were both great fans of rap and hip hop, and they grew up listening to Dizzy Gillespie, Richard Pryor, and other notable names in the entertainment industry.

They came up with the group’s name abruptly one day. The moniker came to Olu randomly during a moment of clarity. In an interview with Creative Loafing, he said the following about the group’s name, EarthGang:

It represents what we’re made of and why we do what we do. It’s for people by the people. Our music is influenced by conscious and subconscious, and we create it to influence the like.

The members of the rap group attended Hampton University, where they started pursuing music seriously. It is here that EarthGang created a strong stance in the entertainment industry.

EarthGang members

EarthGang is a duo, meaning that it is a group of only two rappers. They are Olu and WowGr8. The EarthGang members' bio is quite interesting, considering the circumstances surrounding each while growing up.

Olu (Johnny Venus)

Olu is one-half of the hip hop duo EarthGang. He was born on October 9, 1989, in Atlanta, Georgia, and is 32 years old as of 2021. His real name is Olu O. Fann, but fans know him by his stage name Olu.

EarthGang released their first EP, the mixtape called The Better Party on January 26, 2010. Before they became popular, they were recording music in different places that wouldn't be regarded as studio-like.

For instance, they recorded in various studio spaces that included dorm rooms at Hampton University and even in closets in Atlanta. Fortunately, they got an opportunity to record in Hampton University's Music Recording Technology Jazz studio.

In 2010, the group started touring the United States of America, performing at various festivals like the A3C festival in Atlanta. The music group put together a string of electric releases from 2013, under the backing of J Cole, who had a massive influence at the start of their careers. EarthGang has various albums, extended plays, mixtapes, compilation albums, collaborative albums, and singles.

Is EarthGang part of Dreamville?

Who is EarthGang signed to? EarthGang was signed to J Cole’s Dreamville on August 31, 2017. The duo landed on J Cole’s radar during the early years of their career when he was starting his record label. Olu said the following in an interview with Mass Appeal in 2018 about their first encounter with J Cole.

He reached out, unofficially, way early. He heard about EarthGang [but] we didn't believe it because he didn't directly talk to us. He talked to somebody else who'd talk to us.

Another interesting thing about EarthGang is that they are part of a larger music collective called Spillage Village, with JID, 6lack, and Meraba. Olu and WowGr8 started the hip-hop collective when they were at Hampton University.

Hollywood JB and Jordan Bryant later joined the group. Later, they crossed paths with JID after coming across his Cakewalk mixtape. Also, 6Lack and Mereba would join the collective.
