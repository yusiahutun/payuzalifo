    How to Select the Best Oil-free Sunscreen for Yourself?

    When it comes to choosing an oil-free facial sunscreen, you need to keep a few factors in mind. To find out what they are, take a look below:

    Consider Your Skin Type

    Firstly, you need to be aware of the type of skin that you have. If it’s oily or prone to regular breakouts, then an oil-free face sunscreen is the right fit for you.

    Check for a Non-comedogenic Formula

    Take a look at the product labels and check for an oil-free, non-comedogenic sunscreen. These formulas will not clog your pores, ensuring that you don’t break out and that it doesn’t worsen any existing acne, if present.

    Opt for a Good SPF Level

    When purchasing sunscreen, it’s important that you choose one with an appropriate SPF level, according to your skin’s requirements. We suggest that you go for SPF 30 at minimum, especially if you’ll be using it every day.

    Prioritize Broad Spectrum Protection

    If your sunscreen offers broad-spectrum protection, it means that it will protect your skin from both UVA and UVB rays – this is something that’s very essential.

    Inspect the Ingredients

    Before buying sunscreen, quickly run through the ingredients to check if you may be allergic to something. Also, if you have sensitive skin, fragrance-free options would be a better choice for you.

    Look for Extended Water Resistance

    If you live an active lifestyle, you’re bound to sweat a lot. In this case, good water-resistant sunscreens can come in handy, offering prolonged protection.

    Tips to Effortlessly Apply Oil-free Sunscreen

    Applying any type of sunscreen is a straightforward process, go over the following tips to wear it the right way:

  • Start with skin that’s both clean and dry.
  • Take a dime-sized amount of your favorite sunscreen in your hands.
  • Using a gentle touch, apply the product in an upward motion.
  • Apply some to your neck as well – you can use more sunscreen if required.
  • Give it a few minutes to get fully absorbed into your skin.
  • Lastly, if required, you can use makeup over it.

    What are some essential ingredients to look for in an oil-free sunscreen?

    Zinc oxide or titanium dioxide are two ingredients that offer broad-spectrum protection – look for these in the list of ingredients when purchasing an oil-free sunscreen.

    Can oil-free sunscreen provide adequate sun protection?

    Yes, just like its counterpart that contains oil, oil-free sunscreens can also provide adequate if not better sun protection. However, this varies from product to product.
